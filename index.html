About the Congress

Exploring DNA Across Disciplines

The "Dimensions of DNA: A Multidisciplinary Perspective" congress brings together scientists, researchers, clinicians, and practitioners from diverse fields to explore the multifaceted nature of DNA research and its implications across various domains.

This international gathering aims to foster interdisciplinary dialogue, share cutting-edge research, and address the ethical, social, and practical dimensions of genetic science.

Join us for an enriching experience featuring keynote presentations, panel discussions, workshops, and networking opportunities with leading experts in the field.

Scientific & Technological Advances

  • DNA structure, function, and regulation
  • Genomics, transcriptomics, and epigenetics
  • CRISPR and genome editing technologies
  • DNA sequencing and bioinformatics
  • Synthetic biology and genetic engineering

  • Soil and sediment DNA as bioindicators

  • Ethics in geo-genomic research

Congress Program

Topics of Interest

The congress program covers the following multidisciplinary topics:

  • Scientific and Technological Advances: DNA structure, genomics, CRISPR, sequencing, bioinformatics, synthetic biology, DNA computing
  • Health, Medicine, and Personalized Care: Future of medicine, pharmacogenomics, diagnostics, gene therapy, rare diseases, ethics
  • Education and Public Understanding: Teaching genetics, public engagement, combating misinformation, cultural narratives
  • Forensics, History, and Anthropology: Forensic DNA, ancient DNA, human evolution, genealogy, cultural heritage
  • Ancient DNA in Paleontological Contexts: aDNA recovery, stratigraphy, extinct species, human migration
  • Environmental DNA and Geomonitoring: Biodiversity monitoring, ecosystem change indicators
  • Paleogenomics and Earth History: Evolutionary reconstruction, correlation with geological events
  • Ethics and Access in Geo-Genomic Research: Sampling ethics, ownership of genetic resources

For the detailed schedule, please visit the Schedule section.
